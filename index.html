This section outlines the ancient, medieval, and modern historical sources that reconstruct Haryana's past, focusing on literary texts and key historical events.

Ancient History & Literary Sources

1. Epics and Puranic References

  • Mahabharata: Mentions 'Nakula Digvijaya' detailing Nakula's expedition to Rohtak.
  • Vaman Purana: Refers to the region as "Kuru Jangala".
  • Vaman Purana Details: Specifically outlines 7 forests (Kamyak, Aditi, Vyas, Dvait, Surya, Phalki, Sheet) and 9 rivers (Saraswati, Apaga, Vaithrani, Mandakini, Madhushrava, Drishadvati, Hiranyavati, Kaushiki).
  • Manu Smriti: Refers to Haryana as "Brahmavarta" (The Northern Altar of Brahma / Brahm ki Uttarvedi).

2. Buddhist and Jain Literature

  • Divyavadana (Buddhist): Highlights the prosperity of Agroha and Rohtak; notes Rohtak residents as music lovers.
  • Majjhima Nikaya: Mentions Dhankot (Gurugram) as contemporary to Kurukshetra.
  • Manjusri-mula-kalpa: Describes Shrikantha Janapada and identifies its rulers as Vaishyas.

3. Secular & Foreign Accounts

  • Ashtadhyayi (Panini): Labels the Yaudheya Gana as "Ayudhjivi" (those living by weapons).
  • Ashtadhyayi City Names: Kapishthala (Kaithal), Sarishaka (Sirsa), Kausalya (Tohana), and Kalakuta (Kalka).
  • Harshacharita (Banabhatta): Refers to the area as Shrikantha Janapada with Thanesar as capital.
  • Rajatarangini (Kalhana): Records conquest by Kashmiri King Lalitaditya Muktapida and highlights wealthy merchants of Rohtak.

4. Historical Events & Figures

  • First War of Panipat: Conflict between Babur and Ibrahim Lodi. Local rulers Hasan Khan Mewati and Hamid Khan participated.
  • Banda Singh Bahadur: Established Lohgarh as the first capital for his Sikh forces.
  • George Thomas: Known as "Jahazi Sahib", established his stronghold in Hansi.

Haryana Culture & Folk Arts

1. Prominent Personalities in Literature & Art

  • Pandit Lakhmi Chand: Renowned as the "Shakespeare of Haryana" and "Surya Kavi".
  • Lakhmi Chand Details: Born in Janti village (Sonipat). His guru was Pandit Mansingh.
  • Udaybhanu Hans: Recognized as the First State Poet of Haryana and a pioneer of the Rubai form.
  • Dayachand Mayna: Celebrated as the "John Milton of Haryana".
  • Ali Baksh: Considered the Father of Folk Theater in the state.

2. Traditional Folk Dances

  • Lur Dance: Performed exclusively by women during the Phalgun month just before Holi. Also known as Tunmunia.
  • Ratwai Dance: The traditional dance of the Mewat region, typically performed during the rainy season.
  • Jhoomar Dance: Popularly known as the "Haryanvi Gidda". It is named after a specific ornament worn by married women.
  • Ghoomar Dance: Predominantly performed by women in southern Haryana, especially in districts sharing a border with Rajasthan.

Historical Landmarks, Archaeology & Environment

1. Archaeological Sites

  • Mitathal (Bhiwani): Major Harappan site excavated by Dr. Surajbhan (Panjab University).
  • Mitathal Discoveries: Revealed roads arranged in a chessboard pattern and unique animal artifacts.
  • Brahma Sarovar: Ancient water body excavated by the Kurus; notably mentioned in the accounts of Al-Biruni.

2. Monuments & Landmarks

  • Chor Gumbad (Narnaul): Built by Afghan Jamal Khan.
  • Chor Gumbad Features: Distinct square shape; historically used as a hideout for thieves; colloquially called the "Notice Board of Narnaul".
  • Fairy Queen: Located in Rewari, it is the world's oldest functional steam engine.

3. Environment & Conservation

  • Bhindawas: Recognized as the largest wetland in Haryana.
  • Sultanpur: Achieved the prestigious Ramsar Site status in the year 2021.
  • Vulture Conservation: Jatayu Conservation Breeding Center plays a crucial role in global vulture preservation.

  • Yaudheya Gana: An ancient republic that emerged following the fall of the Mauryan Empire.

  • National Security Guard (NSG): Operational headquarters located in Manesar (Under Union Ministry of Home Affairs).

Current Affairs (Haryana Special)

Governance & Schemes

  • Parivar Pehchan Patra (PPP): Haryana's unique 8-digit family identity document, acting as the primary mechanism for delivering welfare schemes and services directly to eligible families.
  • e-Adhigam Scheme: Launched to distribute tablets to government school students (Classes 10-12) to promote digital education and minimize the digital divide.
  • Chirayu Haryana Yojana: An extension of Ayushman Bharat to provide health cover of Rs 5 lakh to families with an annual income up to Rs 1.80 lakh as verified by the PPP.

Infrastructure & Heritage

  • Rakhigarhi Museum: The ASI and state government are aggressively developing Rakhigarhi (Hisar) into a world-class museum to showcase the largest Indus Valley Civilization site.
  • Global City Project: A massive mixed-land-use project being developed in Gurugram to act as a central business district of international standards.
  • Aravalli Safari Park: Announced to be developed across Gurugram and Nuh districts, aiming to be the largest jungle safari park outside Africa, boosting eco-tourism and conservation.

Sports Achievements

  • Asian Games Contribution: Athletes from Haryana consistently secured over 30% of India's total medals in recent Asian and Olympic games, cementing the state's status as India's sporting powerhouse.
  • Khelo India: Haryana consistently ranks in the top two states in the Khelo India Youth Games medal tally.
